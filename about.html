About You Cannot Have the Stars

The novel grows out of questions about turning points in history: how societies change, how people respond, and how the future shifts when one decision alters the path of history. You Cannot Have the Stars is an alternate past colliding with an unimaginable future — a story of political upheaval, alien contact, and the fragile trust that holds civilizations together.

This website expands on the novel with in-universe articles, fictional lectures, declassified documents, and character writings. The goal is not simply to explain the book, but to create a living archive that invites readers to explore its world more deeply.

Everything on this site is written to avoid spoilers. Readers can freely browse articles, lectures, and background material without fear of ruining the novel’s key revelations.

About the Author

I’ve been reading science fiction for as long as I can remember. The first two books that drew me fully into the genre were Ray Bradbury’s The Martian Chronicles and Charles Sheffield’s Tomorrow and Tomorrow. Since then, I’ve been shaped by a wide range of writers whose work stretches across the speculative spectrum: Peter F. Hamilton, Stephen Baxter, Kim Stanley Robinson, Michael Crichton, and Harry Turtledove among them.

My background in history, which I studied in college, and my love of science fiction has inspired me to create — and ultimately expand — the world of You Cannot Have the Stars. From novels to novellas and short stories, there is no limit to the stories that can be told.
