University Lectures

Explore in-depth discussions and perspectives on political economy, history, and ideology within the world of You Cannot Have the Stars.

  • The 2013 Economic Crash and the Liberty Party: A Nuanced Perspective
    Lecture by Dr. Oscar Newbury, Professor of Economics, UC Berkeley
    An analysis defending Liberty Party ideals amid political and economic turmoil.
  • Market Socialism and Its Future
    Lecture by Dr. Naomi Velasquez, Professor of History, University of Wisconsin–Madison
    A clear explanation of market socialism's role in American history and its evolving future.
  • The Short Depression of 1927: Crisis, Response, and Lessons for Economic Governance
    Lecture by Dr. Naomi Velasquez, Professor of History, University of Wisconsin–Madison
    An in-depth examination of the causes, government response, and lasting lessons of the Short Depression under President Seymour Stedman.
  • WORKNET – Connecting Communities, Cooperatives, and Government
    Lecture by Dr. Henrik Nystrom, Visiting Lecturer in Computer Science, Reed College (on semester appointment from Sweden)
    An introductory exploration of WORKNET, the digital backbone of America’s cooperative system, its history from 1969 to the early 2000s, and its role in connecting citizens, cooperatives, schools, and government for democratic participation and efficient resource management.
