Alternate United States Timeline: 2020–2032

  • November 2020 – President Helen Weiss wins reelection, maintaining stability in domestic and foreign policy. Her administration focuses on cooperative oversight, climate adaptation measures, and technology development, setting the stage for upcoming leadership transitions.
  • January 2021 – Anthropologist Stephen Webber earns his doctorate in early human migration studies. He begins conducting field research, focusing on archaeological sites and human dispersal patterns, establishing himself as a rising expert in his field.
  • November 2022 – Tabitha Bosanac is elected to the House of Representatives for the first time. She quickly begins to develop a reputation for diligence and focus on policy, particularly in science, technology, and infrastructure.
  • November 2024 – Alvin Turner, former Secretary of State under Weiss, wins the presidential election.
  • April 2025 – Dr. Sebastian Adebayo, already recognized as a leading scientist, is appointed to head the Department of Science and Technology in Turner’s administration. He becomes a key figure in advancing national research initiatives.
  • July 14, 2025 – The African Union adopts an updated continental framework for technology, trade, and defense cooperation. A supermajority of nations ratify measures to modernize research sharing, disaster response, and infrastructure projects, signaling Africa’s continued commitment to cooperation as a united and influential bloc.
  • November 20, 2025 – William Obuku of Uganda becomes Secretary-General of the League of Nations.
  • 2026 – Following years of environmental pressure, the Turner administration completes major upgrades to national infrastructure, transitioning significant energy production to wind, solar, and water power. Policies include incentives for cooperative-run energy projects and urban transit electrification, highlighting the administration’s commitment to long-term sustainability.
  • May 2028 – The neural implant known as the MindBeam becomes commercially available, enabling individuals to access, share, and display information directly from their neural interface. The Glass companion device expands visual and collaborative capabilities across professional, educational, and personal use.
  • October 2029 – Danielle Harper, a secret agent with the International Intelligence Agency (IIA), is sent to Rio de Janeiro, Brazil, to infiltrate a laboratory operating under a multinational business facade. The laboratory is working on a highly infectious virus for unknown financiers, initiating a covert operation critical to global security.
  • January 2031 – Following the retirement of the previous chair, Tabitha Bosanac becomes chair of the House Space Technology Research Committee. This position allows her to influence space policy and research funding, bringing emerging technologies into focus at the national level.
  • April 10, 2032 – A mysterious countdown takes over every electronic device in the White House, prompting the president to be taken to safety in an underground bunker and the military to go to Defcon 3.

Between 2020 and 2032, the United States experienced ecological modernization, technological transformation, and the rise of new political figures like Tabitha Bosanac. The Weiss and Turner administrations oversaw cooperative stability and sustainability programs, while the MindBeam altered everyday life. By the early 2030s, with covert intelligence threats and unexplained global anomalies, the stage was set for humanity’s confrontation with forces far beyond Earth.
