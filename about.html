About the Story

You Cannot Have the Stars is a speculative fiction novel that explores a world shaped by a radically different past and thrust into an unprecedented future.

The story begins in a familiar yet fundamentally altered timeline, where the 20th century unfolded along a divergent path. In this alternate history, Eugene V. Debs was elected president in 1920, catalyzing a sweeping transformation of the United States into a global socialist power. Over the next century, the U.S. led advancements in medicine, environmental stewardship, worker rights, and technological development, becoming a beacon for post-capitalist societies across the planet.

But by the 2030s, the world stands at a precipice. Political unrest simmers beneath utopian ideals. Emerging technologies — especially those connected to synthetic memory, neural integration, and global surveillance — have begun to blur the line between liberation and control. Meanwhile, scientists and governments are grappling with discoveries that threaten the foundations of human identity, sovereignty, and survival.

Then, contact is made.

You Cannot Have the Stars weaves together personal stories of eight central characters — politicians, scientists, engineers, survivors — as they navigate the aftermath of first contact with a highly advanced alien civilization. It is a story of power, trauma, trust, betrayal, and the dangerous price of hope.

This website serves as a living archive and immersive companion to the world of the novel. Within these pages, readers can explore the broader universe beyond what appears in the text:

  • Fictional news articles from multiple media outlets provide glimpses into the cultural, political, and social shifts taking place across the globe.
  • University lectures and declassified government memos offer insights into historical divergence, contact protocols, and theoretical alien psychology.
  • Character-written op-eds and speeches illuminate private ideologies and public discourse in an increasingly divided society.
  • Textbook excerpts and field manuals from institutions like the Workers’ University of Chicago or the Martian Colonial Institute add texture to the institutions that define this world.

Every piece on this site is written in-universe and curated to deepen your experience of the story. The goal is not to explain the novel, but to expand it, inviting you to step into the social fabric, philosophical tensions, and shifting power dynamics of a planet in flux.

This is not just a story about aliens. It’s a story about how we define ourselves in the face of the unknown, and what we're willing to sacrifice to preserve our place in the universe.
